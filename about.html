About Me

Who I Am

Hi! My name's Josh McArthur. I'm a fairly young guy and I seem to be passionate about keeping up to date with the latest in the web development world. I try my best with web design, but I really am far more comfortable in a text editor and terminal than Photoshop. I'm currently employed by a local web consultancy here in Wellington named Rabid Technologies, as a developer. Outside of work I enjoy dabbling in obscure corners of the technical world, socializing and exploring Wellington and the surrounding area.

What I Do

I'm a lifelong learner, so in terms of what I'm doing right now - well it could be anything. Here's a break down of what I'll be doing in a typical day though:

  • Using Google Apps throughout the day for managing emails, meetings and documents.
  • Working on between two and four Rails applications throughout the day - some Rails 2.3, and some Rails 3-3.x.
  • Using handy services like memcached, Redis PostgreSQL to manage in the applications I build.
  • Working in my development environment - VIM on OS X, occasionally taking a break to spin up a VM and reminicsing about the good ol' days when I used to use Ubuntu (this was before Unity came along). I'll also occasionally do a tad of brower testing in Windows XP or Windows 7 as well.
  • Setting up servers to host Rails applications securely, usually on Rimuhosting, or taking the cloud-based all-round shortcut, Heroku.
  • Finishing work and heading home for nights full of hacking a range of projects together using Sinatra, Ruby on Rails, or Express.js/Node.js. I've also done a couple of things using this new Meteor thing, and I expect I'll be using that a lot more in the future.
  • Open-sourcing all the work I do on Github, contributing to other open-source projects, writing blog posts and helpful tweets. I also love helping people with web development in person, and generally trying to be helpful.
... whew!

What I Believe In

I open source everything. A lot of the stuff I put out there, as far as I know, is not used by anybody, and that's cool. It's enough for me to know that someday, maybe someone will get some help from something I've created.

Giving back to things that are useful to me - this might be particular causes or campaigns, tools, code, people, projects - whatever, as long as they've given me an equivalent value in a time saving, or lesson learned. For more information about how I give back, check out my record of projects I've donated towards.

Putting in the effort to achieve something is something you'll have to do to earn my respect. I've worked hard to get to where I am, and continue to do so. I absolutely believe that unless you are 100% dedicated to your goals, or at the very least, some sort of short-term objective, you're never going to make much progress in any direction.

Where I Live

I live in Wellington, New Zealand. It's a great city - free WiFi in the CBD, widely known to have the best coffee in New Zealand (if you know where to look), and above all, a real hub of innovation and growth.
